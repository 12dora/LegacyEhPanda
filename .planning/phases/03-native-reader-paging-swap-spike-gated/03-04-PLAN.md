<objective>
Harden the reader's programmatic-jump path — the make-or-break behavior RESEARCH flags as the central
proof (Pitfall 1/2, Open Question 1). Building on Plan 03's compile-clean paging reader, route the three
programmatic writers (autoplay `.next`, slider `setPageIndex`, tap-to-turn `index ± offset`) through the
shared `PageModel` with the `performingChanges` feedback guard so a programmatic write lands on the target
page without re-firing the reducer fan-out or fighting the scroll animation, and clamp every write to the
data source bounds. Confirm the full gesture-coexistence parity (D-09, success criterion #3): paging
frozen under zoom, pan while zoomed, RTL-aware edge tap-to-turn. Add throwaway landed-id logging so the
go/no-go has evidence (not vibes) for off-by-one fidelity.

Purpose: the shared index is both read (scroll → progress) and written (autoplay/slider/tap/resume); an
unguarded two-way binding is the proven feedback-loop / off-by-one hazard. This wave makes the reader's
programmatic jumps trustworthy before the go/no-go and removal. Output: a fully-parity reader on stock
paging, with logged jump fidelity; SwiftUIPager still declared (rollback-safe) until Plan 05.

Implements D-09 (gesture-under-zoom full parity), D-07 (writers on the shared index), D-05
(scrollDisabled zoom-freeze), D-10/D-11, and the RESEARCH § Common Pitfalls 1/2 guards.
</objective>
