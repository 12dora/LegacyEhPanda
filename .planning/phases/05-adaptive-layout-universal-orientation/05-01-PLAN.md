<objective>
Introduce the owner-provided `DeviceType` enum and reshape `DeviceClient` from the four-member
window/idiom struct down to a single injected `deviceType()` fact (D-01/D-03). Swap every reducer /
navigation consumer of `deviceClient.isPad` to `deviceClient.deviceType() == .pad`, preserving
device-class semantics (parity — NOT a size-class conversion).

Purpose: This is the foundation for the whole phase's idiom migration — later view plans read
`deviceClient.deviceType()` for their `DeviceUtil.isPad`/`isPhone` swaps, so `DeviceClient` must be
reshaped first. It also removes the dead `absWindowW`/`absWindowH`/`touchPoint` members (no
consumers) and severs `DeviceClient`'s dependency on `DeviceUtil` and `TouchHandler`.
Output: `DeviceType.swift`; a reshaped `DeviceClient`; 6 idiom-consumer files updated.
</objective>

<assumption_delta_decision>
Primary noun: device identity.
Decision: **promote** — `DeviceType` (the enum) becomes the single device-identity representation;
the `isPad`/`isPhone` boolean idiom is demoted to a derived `deviceType() == .pad` check, not kept
alongside. Owner-locked (D-03); parity-preserving (device-class semantics retained, no size-class
substitution). Rationale: one representation for device class removes the ambiguity of two parallel
idioms and confines the sole static (`DeviceType.current`) to `DeviceClient.live`.
Optional invariant (informational, non-blocking): a later grep gate in 05-10 asserts no consumer
reads a device boolean or `DeviceUtil` static — the only residual is `deviceClient.deviceType()`.
</assumption_delta_decision>
